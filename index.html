About

I am a Ph.D. candidate at Southeast University. My research focuses on robotic perception, LiDAR odometry, SLAM, multi-sensor fusion, and air–ground collaborative 3D reconstruction. I am particularly interested in robust localization and mapping in large-scale, geometrically degenerate, and GPS-denied environments.

My recent work explores adaptive LiDAR odometry, degeneration-resistant registration, visual–LiDAR fusion, continuous-time trajectory estimation, and collaborative mapping for UAV–UGV systems.

Research Projects ----- SLAM | Sensor fusion | Object Detection

Air–Ground Collaborative SLAM

Developed a UAV–UGV collaborative robotic system for cooperative localization and true-color 3D reconstruction, integrating multi-sensor platform calibration, wireless inter-robot communication, and real-time collaborative SLAM for mapping, localization, and autonomous obstacle avoidance.

LiDAR–Vision Fusion-Based Intelligent Road Inspection System

Developed a public-bus multi-sensor mapping system for urban elevated roads, integrating GPS, LiDAR, and vision sensors to support road surface reconstruction, road distress localization, dynamic object removal, and quantitative distress area estimation through LiDAR–camera SLAM and visual detection fusion.

AVP-SLAM Localization System Development

Led the development of a LiDAR-based autonomous valet parking system using a pre-built global map of a multi-level underground parking garage, including high-resolution 3D point cloud mapping, pure LiDAR relocalization, and adaptive LiDAR odometry for different parking environments.

Mining Area Volume Estimation Based on UAV LiDAR SLAM

Led the development of a UAV-based LiDAR–camera data acquisition and mapping system for mining-area reconstruction and stockpile volume estimation, involving LiDAR–camera–IMU fusion mapping, point cloud densification, region-of-interest cropping, and volumetric analysis.

Intelligent Vehicle Perception in Low-Visibility Conditions

Led the development of a visible–infrared bimodal perception system for long-range traffic target detection, covering vehicles and pedestrians within 300 m. The project involved building the bimodal sensing platform, collecting and annotating a visible–infrared traffic object detection dataset, and designing the Dual-YOLO bimodal detection network.

Handheld Device-Based Cave Mapping for UAV Navigation

Developed a portable cave-mapping workflow using handheld sensing devices to construct accurate 3D cave maps, providing prior environmental information and navigation support for UAV operation in GPS-denied and structurally complex underground environments.

Large-Scale Resort Scenic Area Mapping for Autonomous Sightseeing Vehicles

Built large-scale 3D maps of resort and scenic-area environments to support localization, navigation, and deployment of autonomous sightseeing vehicles, with emphasis on robust mapping across open roads, pedestrian areas, and visually complex outdoor scenes.

Current Research

Real-World True-Color 3D Mapping

I am currently working on real-world true-color 3D mapping systems that integrate geometric reconstruction and visual appearance modeling. The goal is to build high-fidelity, scalable, and field-deployable 3D maps for robotic navigation, scene understanding, and digital-twin applications.

Heterogeneous Multi-Robot Mapping

I am developing air–ground coordinated mapping methods for heterogeneous UAV–UGV systems, focusing on cooperative localization, cross-platform map alignment, multi-view reconstruction, and robust operation in large-scale, GPS-denied, and geometrically challenging environments.
